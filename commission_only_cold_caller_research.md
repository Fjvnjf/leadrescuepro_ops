# Commission-Only Cold Caller Research: Bangladesh vs Foreign Markets
## $100 per Closed Deal, No Base Salary

---

# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Bottom Line Up Front:** Commission-only cold calling at $100/deal is NOT realistically sustainable as a pure commission-only model in any market studied, but can work as a SUPPLEMENTAL income model in Bangladesh and the Philippines with the right structure. The most realistic path forward is a hybrid model with a small base salary + commission, or commission-only with training wheels (stipend during ramp-up).

---

# MARKET-BY-MARKET ANALYSIS

## 1. BANGLADESH

### 1.1 Is Commission-Only Common or Rare?
**RARE.** Commission-only roles are extremely uncommon in Bangladesh. The culture strongly favors stable base salaries. Most Bangladeshi workers — especially in call centers/BPO — expect a fixed monthly salary. Commission-only is viewed with deep suspicion as "too risky" or a "scam."

The few commission-only arrangements that exist are typically:
- Small-scale real estate agents
- Insurance agents (very niche)
- Some freelancing platforms (Upwork/Fiverr) where Bangladeshis compete globally

In the cold calling space specifically, it is virtually unheard of for a Bangladeshi to accept pure commission-only.

### 1.2 What Do Experienced Cold Callers Expect to Earn?
- **Base + Commission Model (typical BPO):** 15,000–25,000 BDT/month base ($135–$225 USD) + small commissions or performance bonuses
- **Pure Base (entry-level):** 12,000–18,000 BDT/month ($108–$162 USD)
- **Commission-Only Expectation:** Bangladeshi callers would need to see a realistic path to 30,000–40,000 BDT/month ($270–$360 USD) to justify the risk — which at $100/deal means 3–4 closed deals/month

### 1.3 What Compensation Models Actually Work?
- **Base + Commission Hybrid:** The gold standard. A modest base of 10,000–15,000 BDT ($90–$135) plus $50–70/deal commission is the most effective recruitment model.
- **Weekly Stipend + Commission:** Paying a small weekly amount (like $25/week) to cover basic expenses while they ramp up is more successful than pure commission-only.
- **Tiered Commission Structure:** $70 for first 5 deals, $100 for 6–10, $120 for 10+ can be motivating.

### 1.4 Best Platforms for Finding Commission-Only Callers
- **Bdjobs.com:** Largest job platform. Ads explicitly stating "commission-only" will get very few applicants.
- **Facebook Groups:** "Freelancers of Bangladesh," "BPO Jobs Bangladesh," "Digital Marketing Bangladesh" — better for finding freelancers open to performance-based work.
- **Upwork/Fiverr:** Bangladeshis on these platforms are already freelancing and more open to outcome-based pay, but competition is global.
- **LinkedIn:** Growing but less effective for cold calling roles.
- **University student groups:** Students in Dhaka from private universities (NSU, BRAC, IUB) are more open to risk and flexible work.

### 1.5 Success Stories
**Limited evidence.** Very few documented cases of Bangladeshi commission-only cold calling teams. What does exist:
- Some real estate/lead generation agencies use Bangladeshi cold callers on base+commission, but rarely commission-only.
- A few small-scale operations on Upwork where Bangladeshis take commission-based lead gen projects, but these are individual freelancers, not teams.

### 1.6 What Makes $100/Deal Attractive or Unattractive?
- **ATTRACTIVE:** $100 is roughly 11,000 BDT — about 2/3 of an average monthly salary. So just 2 deals/month beats most entry-level full-time jobs. For a part-time person, even 1 deal/month is meaningful supplemental income. Low cost of living makes $100/deal genuinely significant.
- **UNATTRACTIVE:** The uncertainty. "How long until I close my first deal?" Without a clear ramp, people fear working for weeks or months unpaid. Payment trust issues — will the employer actually pay? Many have been burned.

**NET ASSESSMENT for Bangladesh:** $100/deal is economically attractive IF deals close within 2–3 weeks of starting. The problem isn't the rate — it's the trust gap and cultural preference for stability. Hybrid model (small base + commission) is the realistic path.

## 2. PHILIPPINES

### 2.1 Is Commission-Only Common or Rare?
**UNCOMMON but more acceptable than Bangladesh.** The Philippines has a massive BPO culture with 1.3M+ workers. Commission-only exists in:
- Real estate VA roles
- Sales development representative (SDR) freelance roles
- Some insurance sales

However, for cold calling specifically, commission-only is still rare. The Philippine labor code and cultural norms favor base salaries. Most Filipino VAs expect $3–8/hour or $400–$1,200/month base.

### 2.2 What Do Experienced Cold Callers Expect to Earn?
- **Experienced Cold Caller (Base + Commission):** $600–$1,200/month base + $50–200/deal commission
- **Entry-Level:** $400–$600/month base
- **Top Performers (Appointment Setters):** $1,500–$2,500/month total compensation
- **Commission-Only Expectation:** Filipinos would need to see $800–$1,500/month realistic earnings to go commission-only, meaning 8–15 deals/month at $100 each

### 2.3 What Compensation Models Actually Work?
- **Base + Commission:** The standard. $400–$600 base + $50–100 per qualified appointment or closed deal.
- **Draw Against Commission:** Employer advances the equivalent of a base salary, then recovers from future commissions. More acceptable in PH than Bangladesh.
- **Commission-Only with Guaranteed First Month:** Some agencies pay a flat training/ramp-up amount for Month 1, then switch to pure commission.

### 2.4 Best Platforms for Finding Commission-Only Callers
- **OnlineJobs.ph:** The most popular platform for finding Filipino VAs. You'll find more willingness to negotiate commission structures here.
- **VirtualStaff.ph:** Growing competitor, similar audience.
- **Facebook Groups:** "Filipino Virtual Assistants," "Online Filipino Freelancers," "VA Bootcamp Philippines" — active communities.
- **Upwork/FreeUp:** More competitive but higher quality.
- **LinkedIn:** Effective for experienced BPO professionals looking to go freelance.

### 2.5 Success Stories
Several documented cases, especially in real estate wholesaling and appointment setting:
- Real estate investors routinely hire Filipino cold callers/appointment setters for $3–5/hour + small bonuses, though rarely pure commission.
- Some SaaS companies use Filipino SDRs on base+commission.
- **Pure commission success is mostly in industries with high conversion rates and short sales cycles** (e.g., solar appointment setting, Medicare/Life insurance leads).
- A few agencies have built 10+ person commission-only Filipino cold calling teams, but they typically offer a small base during training.

### 2.6 What Makes $100/Deal Attractive or Unattractive?
- **ATTRACTIVE:** $100 is worth a lot in the Philippines (~5,600 PHP). 5 deals/month = 28,000 PHP, which exceeds many entry-level BPO salaries. English proficiency is high. Strong sales culture and phone etiquette.
- **UNATTRACTIVE:** Filipinos are savvy — they know their market value. Many can get $5–8/hour doing non-sales VA work with zero risk. They compare opportunity cost: "Why gamble on commission-only when I can earn guaranteed $600/month doing admin work?" $100/deal means they need 6+ deals/month just to match a safe admin VA job.

**NET ASSESSMENT for Philippines:** Filipino callers are the MOST VIABLE for commission-only among all markets studied — but even here, you'll struggle to retain people without at least a training stipend. Draw-against-commission or a $200–300/month base is the realistic minimum. The quality-to-cost ratio makes Philippines the strongest candidate market.

## 3. SOUTH AFRICA

### 3.1 Is Commission-Only Common or Rare?
**UNCOMMON but not unheard of.** South Africa has a strong sales culture, and commission-only roles exist in:
- Insurance sales (very common — Sanlam, Old Mutual, Liberty agents often work commission-only after licensing)
- Real estate
- Car sales
- Direct sales/mlm

For remote cold calling, commission-only is rare. South African labor law (Basic Conditions of Employment Act) makes it difficult to engage someone with zero guaranteed pay — they may be classified as employees entitled to minimum wage.

### 3.2 What Do Experienced Cold Callers Expect to Earn?
- **Call Center Agent (Base):** R5,000–R8,000/month ($270–$430 USD)
- **Experienced Sales Agent:** R8,000–R15,000/month base ($430–$800)
- **Top Cold Callers (Lead Gen):** R12,000–R20,000/month total ($645–$1,075)
- **Commission-Only Expectation:** Would need R15,000–R25,000/month ($800–$1,340), meaning 8–13 deals at $100

### 3.3 What Compensation Models Work?
- **Base + Commission:** Standard. R5,000–R8,000 base + R500–R1,500 per deal.
- **Commission-Only (Insurance Model):** Works when the commission percentage is substantial (20–50% of first-year premium). $100 flat doesn't map well to this model.
- **Retainer + Commission:** A small retainer of R3,000–R5,000 keeps people committed.

### 3.4 Best Platforms
- **LinkedIn:** The primary professional platform in SA.
- **Pnet.co.za / CareerJunction:** Major job boards.
- **Gumtree.co.za:** Surprisingly active for sales and freelance roles.
- **Facebook Groups:** "South African Freelancers," "Remote Work South Africa."
- **CallForce / Merchants:** Large BPO recruiters (but they handle benefits/taxes).

### 3.5 Success Stories
Limited in remote commission-only cold calling. What exists:
- Insurance commission-only agents are common domestically, but that model relies on selling to warm South African networks, which doesn't translate to cold calling foreign prospects.
- Some SA freelancers on Upwork doing lead gen, but usually hourly.

### 3.6 What Makes $100/Deal Attractive or Unattractive?
- **ATTRACTIVE:** R1,860/deal is meaningful. 5 deals = R9,300, above minimum wage. Good English, neutral accents valued by US/UK employers. Strong sales instinct in the culture.
- **UNATTRACTIVE:** SA cost of living is higher than Bangladesh/Philippines, making pure commission riskier. Internet/electricity costs (load shedding!) are real. Skilled SA cold callers can get hired locally with full benefits, so commission-only remote work is a harder sell unless the per-deal rate is very high.

**NET ASSESSMENT for South Africa:** Not cost-competitive for commission-only at $100/deal. The base salary expectations and cost of living mean $100/deal only works for very part-time supplementary income. You'd need 8–10 deals/month for someone to go full-time, which is a big ask.

## 4. KENYA

### 4.1 Is Commission-Only Common or Rare?
**RARE but growing in the gig economy.** Kenya has a young, educated, English-speaking workforce. Commission-only is uncommon in formal employment but exists in:
- M-Pesa agent networks
- Insurance sales
- Some BPO/outsourcing (mostly hourly)

The Kenyan job market is tough, which can make commission-only more appealing than in SA — people are hungrier for opportunity.

### 4.2 What Do Experienced Cold Callers Expect to Earn?
- **Call Center Agent (Base):** KSh 25,000–40,000/month ($195–$310 USD)
- **Experienced Sales Agent:** KSh 40,000–70,000/month ($310–$540)
- **Commission-Only Expectation:** Would need KSh 50,000–80,000 ($385–$620), meaning 4–6 deals at $100

### 4.3 What Compensation Models Work?
- **Base + Commission:** KSh 20,000–35,000 base + commission.
- **Commission-Only with Data/Phone Stipend:** Covering airtime and internet costs makes commission-only more palatable.
- **Weekly Payouts:** Immediate payment upon deal close is crucial for trust in the Kenyan market.

### 4.4 Best Platforms
- **BrighterMonday / MyJobMag:** Major Kenyan job boards.
- **Facebook Groups:** "Digital Marketing Kenya," "Freelance Kenya," "Remote Jobs Kenya" — very active.
- **WhatsApp Groups:** Huge job-sharing networks. Getting into the right WhatsApp groups is key.
- **LinkedIn:** Growing rapidly in Kenya.
- **Upwork:** Growing Kenyan freelance community.

### 4.5 Success Stories
- AJIRA Digital Programme (government initiative) has trained thousands in digital/remote work — some have moved into lead gen.
- Some Kenyan VA agencies place cold callers, but usually hourly: $3–5/hour.
- A few solar and fintech companies use Kenyan sales agents on small base + commission.
- **Pure commission cold calling teams in Kenya are rare but not impossible** — it's a relatively untapped market.

### 4.6 What Makes $100/Deal Attractive or Unattractive?
- **ATTRACTIVE:** $100 = ~KSh 13,000. Just 3 deals/month is KSh 39,000 — above average call center salary. 4 deals = comfortable middle-class income. Young, motivated workforce. Excellent English. Similar timezone to Europe/Middle East. Low cost of living.
- **UNATTRACTIVE:** Trust is the #1 issue. Many Kenyans have been scammed by "work from home" schemes promising commissions that never materialize. Internet reliability can be an issue outside Nairobi. Power outages. Payment infrastructure — receiving international payments can be complex (though M-Pesa helps).

**NET ASSESSMENT for Kenya:** Surprisingly promising if you can solve the trust and payment infrastructure issues. The math works: 3–4 deals/month = good income. But you MUST establish trust through consistent, timely payments and ideally provide a small stipend for the first month. Weekly payouts via a service that reaches M-Pesa would be a game-changer.

## 5. JAMAICA

### 5.1 Is Commission-Only Common or Rare?
**RARE.** Jamaica has a significant BPO sector (50,000+ workers) serving US companies, but virtually all are hourly or salaried. Commission-only is almost non-existent in the formal sector. It occasionally appears in:
- Tourism/travel agent commissions
- Some real estate
- Direct sales (very small)

### 5.2 What Do Experienced Cold Callers Expect to Earn?
- **Call Center Agent (BPO):** JMD 80,000–120,000/month ($515–$775 USD) — but these are on-site roles with benefits
- **Remote Freelance:** $4–8/hour is the expectation
- **Commission-Only Expectation:** Would need $600–$1,000/month, meaning 6–10 deals at $100

### 5.3 What Compensation Models Work?
- **Hourly + Bonus:** The standard. $4–6/hour + small performance bonus.
- **Base + Commission:** A base equivalent to JMD 60,000–80,000 ($385–$515) + commission.
- **Commission-Only:** Very difficult sell. Would need a lengthy track record of payment reliability.

### 5.4 Best Platforms
- **CaribbeanJobs.com:** Primary job board.
- **LinkedIn:** Growing professional network.
- **Facebook Groups:** "Jamaican Freelancers," "Remote Work Jamaica."
- **Upwork/Fiverr:** Small but growing Jamaican freelance community.

### 5.5 Success Stories
- Some Jamaican VAs work for US real estate investors doing cold calling, but almost always hourly ($5–8/hour).
- The established BPO sector (itel, Sutherland, Xerox/Conduent) provides stable employment with benefits, making commission-only a hard sell.
- Very few if any documented commission-only cold calling teams based in Jamaica.

### 5.6 What Makes $100/Deal Attractive or Unattractive?
- **ATTRACTIVE:** Native English speakers with neutral/North American-friendly accents. Same/similar timezone to US East Coast. Culturally aligned with US markets — Jamaican callers can build excellent rapport with American prospects. Strong sales personality.
- **UNATTRACTIVE:** Cost of living is HIGHER than Philippines/Bangladesh/Kenya. $100/deal = ~JMD 15,500, which is decent but not transformative. Existing BPO jobs pay $500–$775 with benefits and stability. The opportunity cost calculation doesn't favor commission-only. Internet reliability varies.

**NET ASSESSMENT for Jamaica:** Jamaican callers are QUALITATIVELY excellent — possibly the best accent/cultural fit for US markets among all studied. However, they are cost-prohibitive for commission-only at $100/deal. You'd need 7–8 deals/month to match their existing options. Jamaica works better as a premium hourly market ($5–8/hour) or with a strong base+commission structure. For commission-only, it's the weakest fit economically.

## 6. INDIA

### 6.1 Is Commission-Only Common or Rare?
**RARE in formal employment, present in some sectors.** India's massive BPO sector (1.4M+ workers) is overwhelmingly salary-based. Commission-only does exist in:
- Insurance agents (LIC, private insurers — many are commission-only)
- Real estate brokers
- Some edtech sales (though this collapsed post-COVID)
- Direct sales/mlm

For remote cold calling with US/UK clients, commission-only is very rare.

### 6.2 What Do Experienced Cold Callers Expect to Earn?
- **BPO Cold Caller (Base):** ₹15,000–25,000/month ($180–$300 USD)
- **Experienced Sales Agent:** ₹25,000–50,000/month ($300–$600)
- **Top Cold Callers (US Process):** ₹40,000–80,000/month ($480–$960)
- **Commission-Only Expectation:** Would need ₹40,000–60,000 ($480–$720), meaning 5–7 deals at $100

### 6.3 What Compensation Models Work?
- **Base + Incentive:** The industry standard. Fixed salary + performance-linked variable pay (PLVP).
- **Retainer + Commission:** A modest retainer of ₹10,000–15,000 ($120–$180) + commission per deal.
- **Commission-Only with Minimum Guarantee:** Employer guarantees a minimum payout for the first 2–3 months.

### 6.4 Best Platforms
- **Naukri.com / Indeed India / Shine.com:** Major job boards.
- **LinkedIn:** Very active Indian professional network.
- **Upwork/Freelancer:** Large Indian freelance community, more open to commission structures.
- **Facebook Groups:** "Freelancers India," "Remote Jobs India."
- **Internshala:** For students/early career willing to take risks for experience.

### 6.5 Success Stories
- Some SaaS companies use Indian SDRs on base+commission for US market outreach.
- A few real estate wholesaling operations run Indian cold calling teams, but almost universally with a base salary.
- The edtech boom (BYJU'S, Unacademy) created thousands of sales roles — but these were base+commission, and the sector crashed.
- **Pure commission cold calling teams in India are extremely rare** because the formal BPO sector offers stable alternatives.

### 6.6 What Makes $100/Deal Attractive or Unattractive?
- **ATTRACTIVE:** $100 = ~₹8,400. 5 deals/month = ₹42,000 — above average BPO salary. India has enormous English-speaking talent pool. Low cost of living outside Tier 1 cities. Huge number of hungry, ambitious workers.
- **UNATTRACTIVE:** Accent can be a barrier for US markets (though neutral-accent training exists). The established BPO sector offers stability — why gamble? Payment trust issues are significant. Cultural expectation is that "real jobs" come with a salary. India also has strict labor laws that make commission-only arrangements legally gray (contractor vs employee classification).

**NET ASSESSMENT for India:** India has the LARGEST talent pool and good economics at $100/deal (5 deals = solid income), but the accent barrier for US cold calling is real, and the cultural preference for stable salaries is strong. Very hard to build a commission-only team at scale. A hybrid model (small retainer + $70–100/deal) could work for a scrappy, hungry team — possibly targeting Indian freelancers who already work on Upwork/platforms. Tier 2–3 cities (Jaipur, Lucknow, Indore) have lower cost expectations and may be more willing.

# WHAT ACTUALLY WORKS: RECOMMENDED MODELS

## The Brutal Truth
Pure commission-only at $100/deal with zero base is unrealistic as a PRIMARY employment model in ANY market studied. Anyone competent enough to close deals consistently will quickly realize they can get a guaranteed salary elsewhere. You'll attract only the most desperate or the most inexperienced — who will struggle to close deals, creating a vicious cycle.

## The Hybrid Model That Could Work

### Option A: Training Stipend + Commission (Most Realistic)
- **Month 1:** $150–200 fixed stipend + $50/deal (learning phase)
- **Month 2:** $100 fixed + $75/deal (ramp up)
- **Month 3+:** Pure commission at $100/deal or optional small base ($50/month + $90/deal)
- **Target Markets:** Bangladesh, Kenya, Philippines

### Option B: Draw Against Commission
- Advance equivalent of $200–300/month
- Recover from first commissions
- Once draw is "repaid," caller keeps 100%
- Works best in: Philippines, India (Tier 2–3 cities)

### Option C: Tiered Commission with Low Base
- $100–150/month fixed base (covers internet/phone costs)
- $70/deal for first 5 deals
- $100/deal for deals 6–10
- $120/deal for 10+
- Airtime/data stipend included
- Works best in: All markets

### Option D: Team Lead Model
- Find ONE experienced closer who gets a base salary ($400–600/month)
- That person recruits/manages 3–5 commission-only callers who work under them
- Team lead trains, quality-checks, and provides local trust/credibility
- Works best in: Philippines, Kenya, Bangladesh (via university networks)

# KEY FACTORS FOR MAKING COMMISSION-ONLY WORK

1. **Payment speed is everything:** Pay within 24–48 hours of deal close. Weekly payouts build trust. Delayed payments kill the model instantly.

2. **Transparent tracking:** Provide a live dashboard or shared sheet showing every deal, every commission earned and paid. Suspicion of being cheated is the #1 killer.

3. **Provide the leads and dialer:** Never expect commission-only callers to buy their own leads or dialer. The more investment you require from them, the worse your applicant pool.

4. **Short sales cycle:** Commission-only only works if deals close FAST (within 1–2 weeks of first call). If your sales cycle is 30+ days, commission-only will fail because callers starve before their first payout.

5. **Local payment methods:** bKash (Bangladesh), M-Pesa (Kenya), GCash/PayMaya (Philippines), Paytm/UPI (India). International bank transfers are slow and expensive.

6. **Start small, pay fast, build reputation:** Run a pilot with 2–3 callers, pay them IMMEDIATELY and CONSISTENTLY for 2–3 months. Let them become your evangelists. Word of mouth in these communities (especially Facebook/WhatsApp groups) is how you scale.

7. **Script and training must be excellent:** Commission-only callers have zero patience for disorganization. Provide proven scripts, call recordings for training, objection handling guides, and regular feedback.

# REAL SUCCESS STORIES (What Exists)

1. **Real Estate Wholesaling:** Several US real estate investors run Filipino appointment-setting teams on near-commission-only (small weekly stipend + $50–150 per closed deal). Success depends on high-quality leads and short cycles.

2. **Solar/Home Services:** Some agencies use Kenyan and Filipino cold callers to book solar consultations. These work because the appointment-to-deal conversion is high and predictable.

3. **Medicare/Life Insurance:** Call centers in Philippines and India doing Medicare supplement sales often use base+commission, but some agents go commission-only once they have a book of renewals.

4. **The "Learn Then Earn" Model:** A few agencies recruit inexperienced callers, train them for 2–4 weeks at minimum wage, then transition to commission-only. Those who survive the transition become long-term, high-performing team members. Those who don't self-select out.

**CRITICALLY:** I found NO well-documented examples of sustainable, large-scale (10+ people) commission-only cold calling teams with zero base pay in any market. The model exists in fragments — individual freelancers, small scrappy teams — but nothing at scale.

# FINAL RECOMMENDATION

If you have $500–$1,000/month to invest in building a team:

1. Start with 2–3 Filipino callers on a 90-day ramp: $300/month base + $50/deal for months 1–3, then renegotiate toward higher commission/lower base.
2. Simultaneously test 1–2 Kenyan callers with a similar structure but $200/month base (Kenya's lower cost base).
3. If neither market works, test Bangladesh with local payment (bKash) and a university-student recruitment strategy.

If you have ZERO budget for base salaries: Your most realistic path is finding individual freelancers on Upwork who already work on performance-based projects. But expect high churn and inconsistent results. Commission-only teams at scale without a base salary are a myth — or at least, the evidence for their existence is thin.

The "$100/deal, no base" dream is emotionally appealing but operationally fragile. The markets where $100/deal is economically meaningful (Bangladesh, Kenya) are also the markets where trust in foreign employers is lowest. The markets where trust is higher (Philippines) have more competing opportunities. There's no perfect market — only tradeoffs.
