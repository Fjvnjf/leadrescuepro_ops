# STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP) & TRAINING MANUAL

## Position: Outbound Sales Development Representative (SDR) / Appointment Setter

## Objective: Secure permission from business owners to send a personalized 3-minute video asset (Loom). Do not attempt to sell our services on this call.

---

## 1. Project Overview & Core Mindset

Welcome to the team. At **LeadRescuePro LLC**, we help plumbers capture missed revenue by deploying human-like AI Voice Receptionists to answer after-hours and missed calls.

When making these cold calls, you must maintain a specific psychological framework:

**The Low-Pressure Approach:** We are not hard-selling a meeting or a contract. We are simply offering to send them a short, helpful, 3-minute video analysis of their business or their local competitors.

**Tone of Voice:** Do not use a hyper, enthusiastic "salesperson" voice. Speak calmly, casually, and confidently—like a peer or a consultant who just noticed a problem on their website.

---

## 2. Your Daily Workflow

Every day when you clock in, you will follow these exact steps to ensure maximum calling efficiency:

1. **Log In:** Open the web-based Caller Dashboard and navigate to your assigned lead list.
2. **Review the Lead:** Before dialing, quickly look at the business name, city, and owner/manager name so you can reference them naturally.
3. **Execute Dials:** Aim to complete a minimum of 100 to 150 dials per day.
4. **Update Statuses:** Instantly log the call result using the dashboard buttons.
5. **Report Metrics:** Submit your End-of-Day (EOD) report before logging off.

---

## 3. The Master Cold Call Script

### The Hook (First 15 Seconds)

> "Hey [Prospect Name], it's [Your Name] here. Look, I'll be completely honest with you right out of the gate—this is a cold call. I know you're right in the middle of your workday, but if you give me just 30 seconds to tell you exactly why I called, you can decide if you want to hang up on me. Is that fair?"

*(Pause completely and wait for their response. Most will say "Go ahead" or chuckle).*

### The Problem & Insight

> "Awesome, thank you. I was looking over your Google Business listing and noticed you guys are actively running ads right now. I actually called your front desk yesterday after-hours just to see how your customer intake works, and it went straight to a generic voicemail.

> In the plumbing industry, an after-hours lead usually costs around $100 to generate, and if they hit a voicemail, they typically just call the next business on Google. Most owners we talk to are losing about 20% to 30% of their inbound leads simply because their team is busy or the office is closed."

### The Call to Action (Selling the Video)

> "We actually just built a live system for a similar company that uses a human-sounding conversational AI voice assistant to instantly answer those missed calls, answer questions, and book them straight into the calendar so they never lose money to voicemail again.

> I don't want to pitch you anything right now over the phone, but I recorded a quick 3-minute video showing a live demonstration of how this works and exactly where your site is dropping leads. Can I grab your best email or text number to drop that video link over so you can check it out whenever you have a free minute?"

## 4. Objection-Handling Matrix

### Objection 1: "Just email me your general information."
**Your Response:**
> "I completely understand, and I don't want to spam your inbox with generic brochures. The video I recorded actually pulls up your specific website and phone number to point out the gap, so it's tailored just for you. Is it best to send that directly to your personal email, or is there a better address?"

### Objection 2: "Is this an automated robot/AI thing? We don't like AI."
**Your Response:**
> "I totally get the skepticism—most chatbots and robocalls sound incredibly robotic and annoying. This is a completely new conversational voice tech that actually breathes, pauses, and sounds identical to a real front-desk receptionist. That's exactly why I want to send the video, so you can hear how human it sounds for yourself. What's the best email to drop that link into?"

### Objection 3: "We don't miss calls / Our front desk handles everything."
**Your Response:**
> "That's awesome to hear, having a great team upfront is rare. The video actually focuses specifically on what happens at 8:00 PM, 2:00 AM, or during peak hours when lines are completely tied up. It's just a 3-minute breakdown of how to catch those overflow leads. Can I drop it to your email just so you have it as a resource?"

### Objection 4: "I don't have time right now."
**Your Response:**
> "Completely understand, I know you're running a business. That's actually why I'm not asking for a meeting right now. I just want to drop a link to your email so you can watch the 3-minute overview at 10:00 PM or over the weekend when you're completely free. What email should I send that to?"

## 5. Call Disposition Rules

Every call must be logged immediately after hanging up. Use these exact labels:

| Disposition | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Loom Requested** ✅ | Prospect agreed to receive the video | Double-check spelling of email/phone, note in dashboard, move to follow-up |
| **No Answer / Voicemail** ❌ | No one picked up or went to voicemail | Log as No Answer. Will auto-recycle for follow-up later |
| **Gatekeeper / Call Back** ⏰ | Spoke to receptionist, told to call back later | Log as Call Back. Note the time they suggested to call back |
| **Not Interested / DNC** 🚫 | Firm no or asked to be removed | Mark as DNC immediately. Scrubbed from future campaigns |
